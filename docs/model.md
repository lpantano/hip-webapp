Yes, **anyone from anywhere can join and pay membership fees**, but there are important compliance considerations:

## Membership Structure - Allowed

**Geographic scope:**
- Members can be from any state or country
- Membership fees are allowed for 501(c)(3) nonprofits
- No restriction on where members live

## Critical Compliance Issues

### 1. **Charitable Solicitation Registration**

If you're asking people to join/donate from other states, you may need to register in **each state** where you solicit:

**States requiring registration**: ~41 states
**Cost**: Varies by state ($0-$400/state)
**Trigger**: Soliciting donations/memberships from residents of that state

**Example:**
- You email someone in California asking them to join → May trigger CA registration
- Someone in Texas finds you organically and joins → Generally no registration required

**Exemptions exist for:**
- Very small organizations (typically under $25,000-$50,000/year depending on state)
- Organizations that don't actively solicit in that state

### 2. **Are Membership Fees "Donations" or "Services"?**

**This is crucial for 501(c)(3) compliance:**

**If fees = donations (solicitation registration required):**
- Members get minimal/no benefits
- Tax-deductible for members
- Triggers state registration requirements

**If fees = payment for services (generally no registration):**
- Members get substantial benefits (app access, features, content)
- NOT tax-deductible for members
- Generally avoid state registration requirements
- Must be related to your exempt purpose

## Recommended Structure

**Option 1: Service-Based Membership (Simpler)**
- Membership = access to app/platform/services
- Fees are payment for services, not donations
- Members cannot deduct fees on taxes
- Avoid most state registration requirements
- **Must**: Provide real value/services for the fee

**Option 2: Donation-Based Membership (More Complex)**
- Membership = supporting the mission
- Fees are charitable contributions
- Members can deduct on taxes (if no substantial benefits)
- **Required**: Register in states where you solicit
- Much more administrative burden

## What Revenue is Allowed for 501(c)(3)?

**Your nonprofit CAN have:**
- Membership fees (if related to exempt purpose)
- Program service revenue (app subscription fees)
- Donations
- Grants

**Example nonprofits with paid memberships:**
- Consumer Reports (paid subscriptions)
- Museums (paid memberships)
- Professional associations

## Key Question for Your App

**What does membership provide?**
- Access to app features/tools? → Service-based (simpler)
- Just supporting the mission? → Donation-based (complex)
- Mix of both? → Hybrid (complicated tax treatment)
